# Kid-Friendly Habitat Signboards

## Trail Order
Dragon Sanctuary -> Earthworm Soil -> Caterpillar Nursery -> Butterfly Vivarium -> Frog Pond -> Rainbow Reserve

## Dragon Sanctuary
Brave explorers start here.
Warm up your math powers before entering the root-and-power habitats.

## Earthworm Soil (Cube Roots)
Welcome underground.
Can you find the number that was multiplied by itself three times?
Example: cube root of 1,000,000 is 100, because 100 x 100 x 100 = 1,000,000.
Wiggle challenge: What is the cube root of 27?

## Caterpillar Nursery (Square Roots)
Tiny caterpillars need perfect square leaves.
Find the number that makes the square.
Example: square root of 256 is 16, because 16 x 16 = 256.
Crawl challenge: What is the square root of 81?

## Butterfly Vivarium (Squaring)
Flap into power math.
Squaring means multiplying a number by itself.
Example: 256 x 256 = 65,536.
Wing challenge: What is 12 squared?

## Frog Pond (Cubing)
Jump into 3D math.
Cubing means multiplying a number by itself three times.
Example: 100 x 100 x 100 = 1,000,000.
Splash challenge: What is 5 cubed?

## Rainbow Reserve
You made it.
Collect your rainbow badge for mastering squares, roots, and cubes.
